Mission & Purpose

This guide helps creators, editors, and partners develop clear, consistent, and user-friendly legal information for the people of Pennsylvania. Our primary goal is to make complex legal concepts accessible to everyone, regardless of background or education level.

Core Principles

User-First
Write for the person seeking help, not for legal professionals. Assume the reader is stressed, short on time, and may have limited literacy skills.
Clarity Over Complexity
Use plain language. Explain necessary legal terms simply. Swap jargon for everyday words and define terms you can't avoid.
Action-Oriented
Tell users what steps they can take. Every page should end with a clear next step or call to action.
Accuracy is Paramount
All legal information must be legally accurate and up to date. Always include a "Last Updated" date and content source.
Neutral & Professional
Maintain a respectful, empathetic tone. Avoid sensationalism or political bias.
2
Content Scope

Content decisions should be strategic and focused on our core audience: low-income Pennsylvanians with civil legal needs.

Primary Criteria for Inclusion

Include content only if it meets all four of the following criteria:

  1. Serves a Core Legal Need — addresses a critical civil legal issue (housing, family law, consumer debt, public benefits, employment).
  2. Empowers User Action — helps a person understand rights, navigate a process, or take a concrete step.
  3. Fills a Documented Gap — not already covered comprehensively by a reliable, accessible source. We complement; we don't duplicate unless our version is significantly more user-friendly.
  4. Is Within Our Expertise — we have or can access the legal expertise to create and maintain accurate content.

Decision-Making Questions

Ask these before creating any new content:

  1. Who is the specific user? Describe the person and their situation.
  2. What is the user's goal? What single thing do they need to accomplish?
  3. Is this the right place for this information?
  4. Can we maintain it? If we cannot commit to reviewing it at least annually, do not publish it.
ℹ️
Routing Guide Basic public services (food banks, library cards) → Link to 211 or the relevant agency.
Highly complex, case-specific strategies → Provide basics and a strong referral to legal help.
News or advocacy updates → Consider a blog post or news alert rather than a permanent resource page.

Content Tiers

● High Priority
Tier 1: Core Self-Help
Step-by-step guides, legal process explainers, common forms with instructions. Allows a person to take action without a lawyer.
● High Priority
Tier 2: Know Your Rights & Referrals
Overviews of legal rights and pathways to help. Helps users understand their situation and find the right assistance.
● Lower Priority
Tier 3: Context & Awareness
General legal news, community resource lists, or information about non-legal problems with a legal dimension.
💡
Guiding Principle When in doubt, prioritize Tier 1 and Tier 2. Our unique value is providing actionable, reliable legal information that directly helps people resolve their civil legal problems.

Writing for the Web

Readability

  • Target reading level: 8th grade or lower. Use Hemingway Editor or Microsoft Word's readability checker.
  • Sentence length: Keep under 25 words.
  • Paragraph length: Maximum 3–4 sentences.
  • Use bulleted or numbered lists liberally.
  • Use clear, descriptive headings (H2, H3) to allow scanning.

Page Structure

  • Inverted Pyramid — Start with the most important information (the conclusion or key action). Follow with supporting details. End with background.
  • The One-Thing Test — Every page should answer: "What is the one thing I want the user to know or do after reading this?"
  • Use White Space — Ample white space makes text less intimidating and easier to read.

Active Voice

Use active voice to make sentences clearer and more direct.

Passive (Avoid)Active (Use)
The forms must be completed by you.You must complete the forms.
A hearing will be scheduled by the court.The court will schedule a hearing.
The application can be submitted online.You can submit the application online.
Benefits may be terminated if…Your benefits may end if…

Personal Pronouns

  • Use "you" and "your" to speak directly to the user.
  • Avoid "he/she" or "s/he."
  • Use "they/them" as a singular pronoun when gender is unknown or unspecified.
  • Use "the court," "the judge," "the landlord" — not "he" or "she" — when referring to roles.

Acronyms & Abbreviations

Acronyms can confuse users who are unfamiliar with legal and government terminology. Follow these rules consistently across all content.

General Rules

  1. Spell out the full term on first use, followed by the acronym in parentheses. Example: "Protection From Abuse (PFA) order." After that, you may use PFA alone.
  2. On short pages (under 500 words), spell out the full term every time if you only use it once or twice.
  3. In page titles and headings, use the spelled-out term or most commonly known version. Do not start a heading with an acronym the user has not yet seen.
  4. When writing for non-legal audiences, prefer the plain-language name even when an acronym is widely used internally.
  5. Do not use periods in acronyms (SNAP, not S.N.A.P.).

Legal-Specific Guidelines

Explaining Legal Terms

  • Bold a legal term on its first use and provide a plain-language definition immediately.
  • Example: A complaint is the first legal document filed in a lawsuit. It explains the facts and the reasons the plaintiff is suing the defendant.
  • Avoid Latin terms. Use plain English equivalents where possible.
Avoid (Latin)Use Instead
in loco parentisin place of a parent
pro seself-represented, representing yourself
habeas corpusa court order to release someone from unlawful detention
voir direjury selection process
prima facieat first look, on its face

Citations

  • Keep citations simple and unobtrusive.
  • Format: "Under 23 Pa.C.S. § 4321, …" and link to the statute on the Pennsylvania General Assembly website.
  • Avoid long strings of case citations in public-facing content.

Standard Disclaimer

Include the following on every page with legal information:

⚠️
Required Disclaimer This website provides legal information, not legal advice. We make every effort to ensure the accuracy of the information, but it may not apply to your specific situation. We encourage you to consult with a lawyer for advice about your individual case.
  • Always distinguish between an attorney and a non-attorney (paralegal, navigator, etc.).
  • Clearly state the date of last review on each page.

Formatting & Visuals

Links

Use descriptive link text. Never use "click here."

AvoidUse Instead
To file for custody, click here.Learn more about filing for custody.
Click here for the form.Download PA Form CN-101: Complaint for Custody.
More information here.See the Pennsylvania court's self-help guide.
  • External links (to courts, state agencies, etc.) should open in a new tab to keep PALawHelp.org open.

Calls to Action (CTAs)

Make CTAs clear and action-oriented. Lead with a verb.

  • Find Legal Help
  • Download the Form
  • See if You Qualify
  • Get Help With Your Eviction
  • Request a Fee Waiver

Lists

  • Use bulleted lists for unordered items (options, considerations).
  • Use numbered lists for sequential steps (instructions, processes).
  • Keep list items parallel in structure — start each with the same part of speech.
  • Avoid lists of more than 7–8 items. Break into subgroups with a subheading if needed.

Pennsylvania-Specific Content

Court Names

Use correct, official Pennsylvania court names at all times.

CourtNotes
Magisterial District CourtLowest level of PA courts. Handles minor civil and criminal cases. Judge is called a "Magisterial District Judge" (MDJ).
Court of Common Pleas (CCP)General trial court at county level. Most civil and criminal cases.
Superior CourtIntermediate appellate court. Hears appeals from CCP in most civil and criminal matters.
Commonwealth CourtHears appeals involving state government, administrative agencies, and regulatory matters.
Supreme Court of PennsylvaniaHighest court in the state.

Forms

  • Always specify the correct Pennsylvania form number and name. Example: "PA Form CN-101: Complaint for Custody."
  • Link directly to the form on the appropriate court or county website when possible.
  • Note when forms vary by county.

Geographic Scope

  • Be explicit when information varies by county: "Procedures can differ by county. Contact your local county court for specific rules."
  • Use the site's "Find Your County" functionality prominently when applicable.
  • Do not assume Philadelphia or Pittsburgh rules apply statewide.

Accessibility

All content must meet WCAG 2.1 AAA standards.

RequirementWhat to Do
Alt TextAll images must have descriptive alternative text that conveys the image's meaning, not just its appearance.
Heading HierarchyUse heading levels (H1, H2, H3) in logical order. Never skip levels. Screen readers rely on these for navigation.
Color as MeaningDo not use color alone to convey meaning (e.g., don't say "items in red are required" — add an asterisk or label).
Link ContextLink text must make sense out of context. Screen reader users often tab through links only.
Video / AudioProvide transcripts for all audio content. Provide captions for all video content.
Reading LevelTarget 8th grade or lower. Benefits users with cognitive disabilities, low literacy, and non-native English speakers.
Contrast RatioBody text: minimum 7:1 (AAA). Large text (18pt+ or 14pt bold): minimum 4.5:1.

Content Maintenance

Review Cycle

  • All content must have a "Last Updated" date displayed publicly.
  • Establish a review schedule on publication — at minimum annually for Tier 1 and Tier 2 content.
  • Content owners are responsible for initiating reviews or flagging content that needs legal SME review.
  • Legal changes (new legislation, rule changes, court decisions) may trigger off-cycle review.

Archiving and Removal

  • Outdated content should be archived or removed to prevent user confusion.
  • Do not leave stale content live without a clear notice.
  • If a page is superseded by new content or a new law, redirect the old URL where possible.
  • Tier 3 content (news, alerts) should be reviewed most frequently.
